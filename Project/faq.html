FAQ

Q. How are the species’ status defined?

A. The species’ status, i.e, the conservation status of a species is decided by the International Union of Conservation of Nature (IUCN) Red List. The IUCN Red List has explicit criteria and categories to classify the conservation status of a species, on the basis of their probability of extinction, into one of eight categories.

Q. What is the IUCN Red List?

A. The IUCN Red List is a critical indicator of the health of the world’s biodiversity. Far more than a list of species and their status, it is a powerful tool to inform and catalyse action for biodiversity conservation and policy change, critical to protecting the natural resources we need to protect.

Q. What role can a normal individual play in the conservation of nature?

A. There are various ways by which a normal individual can help save nature. A few of them have been listed below:
Grow native plants Reduce your personal footprint. Drive less, walk more. Do not buy plastic products. Volunteer your time to protect the wildlife in your area. Blackout the Black Market Donate to the organisations who work on conservation

Q. What do organisations do to conserve nature?

A. Organisations work on conservation breeding, habitat preservation, public education, field conservation, supportive research, etc to ensure survival for many of the planet's threatened and endangered species. They address the challenges facing key endangered, threatened, or declining species that are indicators for overall ecosystem health.

Q. Which are the organisations who work on conservation of nature?

A. There are a lot of different organisations worldwide who work solely for the conservation of nature. A few of them are World Wildlife Fund (WWF), IUCN, International Rhino Foundation, The Nature Conservancy, Wildlife Alliance, International Fund for Animal Welfare (IFAW), Panthera, Oceana, Born Free, Rainforest Alliance, International Anti-Poaching Foundation, Tusk Trust, Jane Goodall Institute, The Pew Charitable Trusts, Environmental Investigation Agency, Wildlife Conservation Society.
